How VALUS values {{ ticker }}

Every {{ ticker }} valuation begins with the company's reported free cash flow, then projects ten years of growth using a sector-aware ceiling so highflyers can't compound forever. A WACC discount brings those flows back to today, a terminal value caps long-run growth, and a reverse-DCF tells you what growth rate the current price would require — a sanity check on whether the market is paying for execution that's actually plausible.
