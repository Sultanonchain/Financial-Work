Why this verdict?

How VALUS arrived at this rating
  1. VALUS computes an intrinsic value (DCF + reality reconciliation + Sultan Split + cash premium).
  2. It compares that fair value to the current market price → margin of safety.
  3. The implied growth rate (reverse-DCF) sanity-checks against the sector growth ceiling.
  4. The verdict tier is assigned based on the margin of safety, with a sector-ceiling override for speculative growth.
Priced for Distress MOS > +50% Market is overly pessimistic. Trading well below fair value, often during macro fear or sector rotation.
Priced for Deep Discount MOS +40% to +50% Significantly undervalued — strong signal if VALUS's growth assumptions hold.
Priced for Discount MOS +15% to +40% Trading below fundamental value. Modest opportunity zone.
Priced for Fair Value MOS −10% to +15% Market and VALUS aligned. No clear edge — hold or wait for a better entry.
Priced for Growth MOS −10% to −25% Market paying a growth premium — acceptable if execution delivers above-base growth.
Priced for Excellence MOS −25% to −50% Market expects flawless execution. Limited margin of error if any leg slips.
Priced for Miracle MOS < −50% or implied growth > sector ceiling × 1.2 Market pricing in outcomes fewer than 1% of public companies achieve. Speculative.

Why "Low Confidence"?

VALUS flags a margin of safety as low confidence when its absolute value exceeds ±100%. When the gap between fair value and price is that extreme, the DCF inputs are usually mispriced.

Share-class mismatch Berkshire Hathaway B-shares (BRK-B) trade at ~1/1500th of A-shares. yfinance occasionally returns A-share fundamentals against B-share price, producing a 19,000%+ MOS that's purely a unit error.
Forward-earnings spike When TTM FCF is depressed but next-year estimates are sharply higher, the DCF can use the inflated forward number as base, multiplying by a decade of growth and producing an unrealistic IV.
Leverage non-monotonicity In highly-levered industrials (CCL, F, BA, AAL), higher growth requires more capex and debt — the equity value can actually fall as growth rises. The reverse-DCF binary search converges in unexpected places.
Stale or split-adjusted prices When yfinance hasn't propagated a recent stock split or merger, the price and fundamentals temporarily disagree.

What to do: open the methodology breakdown and check whether Reality Reconciliation, Cash-Rich Premium, or the Sultan Split are the ones doing the heavy lifting. Cross-check against analyst targets and recent filings before relying on the IV.
