Understanding the DCF Model

WACC — Weighted Average Cost of Capital
The discount rate used to convert future cash flows to today's dollars. Higher WACC = lower intrinsic value. Calculated from the company's mix of equity and debt financing costs.
Cost of Equity (CAPM)
The return shareholders require: Risk-Free Rate + Beta × Equity Risk Premium. We use 4.5% risk-free rate and 5.5% ERP.
Stage 1 Growth
FCF growth rate for years 1–5. Defaults to analyst estimates or the 5-year historical FCF CAGR, capped at 25%.
Stage 2 Growth
FCF growth rate for years 6–10. Defaults to half of Stage 1 to reflect slower, more mature growth.
Terminal Growth Rate
The perpetual growth rate beyond year 10. Typically 2–3%, in line with long-run GDP growth.
PV of FCFs
The sum of all 10 projected free cash flows, each discounted back to today at the WACC rate.
PV Terminal Value
The present value of all cash flows beyond year 10, modeled as a growing perpetuity. Often the largest component of DCF value.
Margin of Safety
How far the stock price is from intrinsic value. Positive = trading below IV (potential bargain). Negative = trading above IV. Rule of thumb: buy at 20%+ discount.
⚠ DCF models are highly sensitive to assumptions. Small changes in growth rate or WACC can dramatically change intrinsic value. Use this as one input among many — not as the sole basis for any investment decision.
